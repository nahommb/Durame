Location

Durame is a city located in the south-central part of Ethiopia, approximately 340 kilometers from the capital city of Addis Ababa. The city has a population of around 50,000 people and serves as an important economic hub for the surrounding agricultural region. One notable feature of Durame is its location at the foot of the Hambaricho Mountain range, which is known for its stunningly beautiful landscapes and unique flora and fauna. Visitors to the area can explore the rugged mountain trails, visit local waterfalls, or relax in the shade of the dense forests that cover much of the area. In terms of culture and history, Durame has a rich heritage dating back many centuries. Visitors can explore ancient cave paintings and tombs, visit local museums, or attend celebrations and festivals that showcase the vibrant traditions of the region's ethnic groups.

Durame is a multi-ethnic city, and as such, its population is made up of people from various ethnic groups. The Kembata people are one of the largest ethnic groups in Durame, and they have a strong presence in the city's economic and cultural life. Other ethnic groups represented in Durame include the Gurage, Hadiya, and other ethiopian peoples, among others. Despite their different backgrounds, the people of Durame share a common bond in their pride for their city and their commitment to improving their communities. They are known for their warmth and hospitality towards visitors, and often welcome tourists with open arms. The people of Durame are also hardworking and entrepreneurial, with many small businesses operating throughout the city. Overall, the people of Durame play an important role in the city's vibrant culture and economy, and continue to work together to create a bright future for themselves and their families.

Overall, Durame is a fascinating destination for those interested in exploring the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Ethiopia's southern regions, offering a unique window into this diverse and vibrant country.


Durame is an ideal destination for tourism and investment opportunities. For tourism, there are numerous attractions that make Durame a unique destination. One of the top sites to visit is the Masala hotel, which offers visitors the opportunity to experience the local culture and traditions while staying in one of the beautifully crafted traditional huts. Visitors can engage in activities such as coffee ceremonies, exploring the nearby forest, or visiting the local markets.

Another attraction in Durame is the beautiful Saro bira landscape and lake, which offers boat rides, fishing, and birdwatching opportunities. The lake is home to a variety of bird species, including the African fish eagle, Marabou stork, and many others. In terms of investment opportunities, Durame offers a range of possibilities. The city has fertile agricultural lands, making it ideal for investing in agriculture or agro-processing industries. Coffee is a major cash crop in the area, and there is potential for investment in coffee processing and exportation. Additionally, the city is located along the major highway that connects major cities in the region, making it an ideal location for investment in transportation and logistics infrastructure.
